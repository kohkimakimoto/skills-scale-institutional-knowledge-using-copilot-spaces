# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove blockers, and foster a culture of continuous improvement. They coach the team on Agile practices and ensure process effectiveness.

### Responsibilities
- Organize and facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove obstacles impeding team progress
- Foster adherence to Agile principles and practices
- Track action items from retrospectives and ensure follow-through
- Shield the team from external distractions during sprints
- Coach team members on self-organization and cross-functionality

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Create a collaborative and psychologically safe environment
- Continuously improve team processes and practices
- Ensure transparency and visibility of work

### Typical Communication
- Facilitate daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members to address impediments
- Coordinate with Project Manager on delivery schedules
- Share retrospective insights and improvement actions

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates closely on delivery schedules, risk management, and resource coordination. The Scrum Master focuses on team dynamics and process while the PM handles broader project coordination and stakeholder management.
- **Developers**: Works daily to remove blockers, facilitate effective standups, and create an environment for optimal productivity. Coaches on Agile practices and self-organization.
- **Product Manager**: Helps ensure backlog items are clear and ready for development. Provides feedback on items needing clarification or reprioritization. Facilitates product backlog refinement sessions.

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain clear, accurate documentation for technical and non-technical audiences. They ensure knowledge is accessible, up-to-date, and supports onboarding, collaboration, and customer success.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain technical documentation (API docs, user guides, architecture docs)
- Create onboarding materials and knowledge base articles
- Collaborate with developers and product teams to understand features
- Review and edit documentation for clarity, consistency, and accuracy
- Establish and maintain documentation standards and templates
- Ensure documentation is versioned and synchronized with releases

### Goals
- Enable self-service and reduce support burden through clear docs
- Improve onboarding time for new team members
- Maintain comprehensive and discoverable knowledge base
- Ensure documentation accuracy and currency

### Typical Communication
- Regular syncs with developers to document new features
- Documentation reviews and feedback sessions
- Stakeholder interviews to gather information
- Updates in project channels about doc status

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Partners closely to understand technical implementation details, gather information for documentation, and review technical accuracy of content.
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on user-facing documentation, release notes, and feature descriptions. Ensures documentation aligns with product vision and user needs.
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates documentation deadlines and deliverables within project timelines. Provides documentation status updates in project reports.

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers translate product requirements into intuitive, accessible, and visually appealing user interfaces. They conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and collaborate with development teams to ensure design feasibility and quality.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user interfaces that meet accessibility standards (WCAG)
- Develop and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate implemented features against design specifications

### Goals
- Create intuitive and delightful user experiences
- Ensure accessibility and inclusivity in all designs
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Reduce friction and cognitive load for users

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with product and engineering teams
- User research findings and recommendations
- Design specs and redlines for development
- Usability test results and iterations

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Works closely to understand product requirements, user needs, and business goals. Translates requirements into actionable design solutions and provides user insights from research.
- **Developers**: Collaborates on design feasibility, technical constraints, and implementation details. Provides design specifications and assets, and validates implementations.
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on design deliverable timelines, dependencies, and resource needs. Participates in project planning to ensure adequate time for design iterations.

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze processes, validate scope, and ensure alignment between stakeholders and development teams.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into technical requirements and user stories
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business objectives
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder meetings
- Create process flows, data models, and other analysis artifacts

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business needs across teams
- Reduce rework by clarifying requirements early
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Validate business value delivery

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Clarification meetings with developers and architects
- Business process documentation and presentations
- User acceptance testing (UAT) coordination

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates to understand product vision and translate it into detailed requirements. Helps prioritize features based on business value and feasibility analysis.
- **Developers**: Provides detailed requirements, acceptance criteria, and business context. Answers questions during development and clarifies scope boundaries.
- **Project Manager**: Works together on project scope definition, requirements documentation, and stakeholder communication. Helps assess impact of scope changes.

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and oversee the release process from code freeze to production deployment. They ensure quality gates are met, coordinate cross-team dependencies, and track post-release metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules across teams
- Manage release branches and version control strategy
- Ensure all quality gates and checklists are completed before release
- Coordinate with QA, development, and operations teams
- Monitor deployments and track post-release metrics
- Manage hotfix processes and emergency releases
- Maintain release documentation and communication

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, on-time releases
- Minimize deployment risks and production incidents
- Improve release process efficiency and automation
- Ensure clear communication of release status and impact

### Typical Communication
- Release status updates and go/no-go decisions
- Coordination meetings with cross-functional teams
- Post-release reports and metrics
- Incident communication during release issues

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinates code freeze deadlines, reviews release readiness, and manages release branches. Works with developers to ensure features are release-ready and to triage issues.
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on release scope, feature priorities, and customer communication. Provides release metrics and feedback for product decisions.
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on release planning, timeline coordination, and risk management. Provides release status for project reports and stakeholder updates.
